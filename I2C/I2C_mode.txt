Standard Mode
- In standard mode communcation data transfer rate can reach up 
to maximum of 100kbit/sec.
- Standard mode was the very first mode introduved when first I2C
spec was released.
- Standard-mode deviced, however, are not upward compatible;
They can't communicate with devices of Fast mode or above.

Fast Mode
- Fast mode is a mode in I2C protocol, where devices can receive and 
transmit data up to 400 kbits/s.
- Fast-mode devices are downward-compatible and can communicate with
Standard-mode devices in a - to 100 kbit/s I2C bus system.
- Standard-mode devices, however, are not upward compatible; they should
not be incorporated in a Fast-mode I2C-bus system as they cannot follow 
the hogher transfer rate and unpredictable states would occur.
- To achieve data transfer rate up to 400 kbits/sec, you must put the
I2C device in to fast mode.
